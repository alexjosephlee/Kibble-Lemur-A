Who We Are

The Kibble Kitchen was established in January 2011 by Michelle Duca of Portage, Indiana. The program started to come to life after Michelle started seeing so many request for help with pet food on Craigslist, & speaking with her friends that worked at shelters learning many families were surrendering their pets due to lack of finances. Michelle took the knowledge she learned in her past by working in pet stores, animal shelters and working with various animal rescue groups to create the Kibble Kitchen. The program seemed simple the need was there, and the concept would be to run it as a human food pantry but focusing on pet related items. She spent countless hours to research the internet for other pet food pantries in the US and took each concept to create the beginnings of the pantry.

Hey, you!!! We're looking for a few talented and conscientious volunteer board members to lead and stregnethen our programs for pets and their families. If you can contribute your time, thoughtfulness, and leadership, call Michelle at (219) 947-4400. She looks forward to hearing from you!

Mission & Vision

To promote the health and well-being of companion animals and their families in need, through supplemental nutrition, education, medical assistance, and referral services in the NWI community.

Objectives
• Increased quality of life and well-being of companion animals in our community.
• Increased image and public awareness of Spay & Neuter to reduce over-population, shelter numbers and homeless animals. 
• Reduction of animal surrenders for lack of resources or education. Increased community knowledge of responsible pet ownership qualities. 
• Building a community based network of affordable providers and pet services to assist low income pet owners and seniors.
• All elderly and low income community members to keep their families together by providing resources and referrals during hard times.
• Increase shelter partnerships to problem solve with potential pet owner surrenders by providing support, solutions, resources and referrals to help the pet stay with its family.

Goals
• To saturate the community with positive education to burst myths regarding spay and neuter and responsible pet ownership.
• To increase community relationships and build a strong network of partners in line with the mission.
• To increase donors and grant funding and volunteers.
• To launch and complete a successful capital campaign to gain funding and resources for a physical location.
• To create a strong volunteer appreciation program.

Ultimate Goal

To develop a hub of affordable pet care services in line with our mission, including shelterin, training, wellness, nutrition, education, spay and neuter, and grooming for pets and families in need.
